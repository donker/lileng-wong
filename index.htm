LILENG WONG

Ceramist & Designer

Off we go!

Lileng was captivated by the art of pottery from an early age. Growing up in Kota Kinabalu, Sabah, Malaysia, her first experience with clay was with her father, who worked in a factory that produced red brick. Sometimes, during school holidays she would follow her father to the factory. “I would be left to play with the clay by myself, while my father was busy with his work. Watching him stoke wood into the fire holes was enough to fascinate me, although I was too young to understand the process at the time”

Mom, can I play

A graphic designer by profession, fuelled by an innate desire to create beautiful shapes, she took up part-time pottery lessons at the Kuala Lumpur College of Art. Since then there was no turning back in the journey from making functional wares to sculpturing. At one point she felt clay is just like a messenger to express the feeling she had. “My inspiration came from the nature, animals, people. Events that happened and the journey through it. It is enough to tell a tale.”

During the early years her pieces were mostly influenced by the minimalism of Japanese school. The shape, colours, firing technique and even the theory behind them. It stirred the imagination as she questioned, what is ‘beauty’? What is ‘perfect’?

In 2007 Lileng was accepted by the Goshogawara International Woodfire Festival as one of the artists to participate in the Tsugaru Kanayama Pottery at Goshogawara city, Aomori, located in northern Japan. Her ‘Artist in Residency’ stay brought her not just the experience of working outside her studio, but it also gave her tremendous respect towards the attitude of Japanese craftmanship in art and culture. Established by Mr. Ryoji Matsumiya in 1984, the philosophy, or motivation, is to promote mutual understanding and greater cooperation among potters throughout the world, by encouraging artists to work together and share information freely during the month-long program. This residency is focused on the exchange of techniques and ideas about ceramic art and wood firing.

“The most important lesson learned during my stay, beside the techniques and ideas, is to have that great attitude towards the whole process of creating each piece of work. I remember until now, when it was my turn to take care of the kiln for a firing session Mr. Ryoji explaining to me how before all the modern technologies, It was common to use sight and the colors of the fire to determine the temperature. Hearing the crackling sound of woods is to know when to stock the next load of wood. That moment was magical, it brought back the memory of my dad firing his kiln and I could recall the smell of his workplace…

Since then my work took on a different path and faced a different dimension. Still often stuck with experiments of shapes and ideas, but the experience grew stronger ever...

The journey, a treasure…”.

Beside working in the studio, Lileng participated in many exhibitions in and outside of Malaysia. “The best part of exhibition for my opinion, beside to share my works with others was the interpretation of others of my works... I often found very inspiring...”

Lileng is currently based in Nohèdes, France, high up in the Pyrénées Orientales mountains where she has her ceramics studio, but she also works from Malaysia and The Netherlands.
